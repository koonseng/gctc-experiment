Task Instructions

In this study, you will see sentences that describe processes or patterns from many different fields — such as biology, music, finance, cooking, and engineering.

On each trial, you will see:

A reference sentence at the top, followed by three options (A, B, C).

Your task: choose the option that shares the most similar underlying pattern or relationship with the reference sentence — even though they describe completely different topics.

Important: We are not asking which sentence is about the same topic. We are asking which sentence describes the same kind of process.

Example:

Reference: "A single match ignites dry brush, spreading fire across the entire hillside."

Option A: "A rumour starts with one person and quickly spreads through the entire school."
Option B: "The gardener carefully waters each plant in the greenhouse every morning."
Option C: "Firefighters train for months before their first real emergency."

The correct answer is A — both describe a cascading spread from a single trigger, even though one is about fire and the other is about rumours.

You'll start with 3 practice trials (with feedback), then complete 30 main trials. Work at a comfortable pace — there is no time pressure, but please don't spend more than about 60 seconds per trial.
